aHUS
Registry
An observational, noninterventional, multicenter,
multinational study of patients with aHUS
  • Collects and evaluates safety and effectiveness data for treatment options
  • Assesses long-term manifestations of TMA complications as well as other clinical outcomes, including morbidity and mortality, regardless of treament approach
  • Benefits of your participation
  • Global collaboration with other physicians dedicated to this serious, life-threatening,
    ultra-rare disease
  • Access to regular feedback with Registry updates and findings
  • Contribution to safety monitoring of novel treatments
  • Increased knowledge to better manage the challenge encountered by patients with aHUS
  • Who can contribute ?
  • All physicians managing patients with aHUS, regardless of therapy
  • Who is eligible?
  • All male or female patients of any age diagnosed with aHUS, defined by
  • Clinical diagnosis of aHUS
  • Patients with or without an identified complement regulatory factor genetic abnormality or anti-complement factor antibody
  • ADAMTS13 >5% (if performed)
